CO2 Emissions controlled by GDP 1922-2014

The second map (red) shows the distribution of CO2 emissions across the globe by country and controlled by GDP. We see that in the 1990s most of Asia had by far the highest CO2 per GDP. If we look at the previous map this can be explained by these countries having large CO2 emissions and low GDPs. North America also has reasonably high CO2 emissions per capita throughout the 22 year period. As time progresses, the CO2 emissions per GDP for most of the countries that previously had disproportionately large emissions seem to fall and approach the global average.
